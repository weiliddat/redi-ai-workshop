# AI Workshop

> **AI can write your code. It can also stop you from learning how.**

A 2-hour remote workshop teaching students how to use AI effectively as a learning tool — not just a productivity shortcut.

## Why this workshop exists

Research shows that developers using AI scored 17% lower on conceptual understanding — not because AI is bad for learning, but because _how_ you use it determines whether you actually learn. Students who delegated thinking to AI retained less. Students who stayed actively engaged — questioning, writing, reasoning — learned just as well as those without AI, sometimes faster.

This workshop gives participants the mindset and practical approach to use AI without losing the skills they're building.

## How it works

The workshop follows a deliberate arc:

1. **Set the stage** — Introduction to the workshop, the facilitator, what AI can and can't do
2. **Experience the problem** — Students try AI on a task with no guidance (the "cold start")
3. **Name the pattern** — Present the research. Students just felt the struggle — now they see the data
4. **Teach the approach** — Live demos of learning-friendly AI patterns
5. **Apply and compare** — Retry the task with intention. The before/after contrast teaches itself
6. **Lock it in** — Practical do's and don'ts they can take away
